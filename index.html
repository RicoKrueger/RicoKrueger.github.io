About

I am an Associate Professor in the Intelligent Transportation Systems Section of the Division for Transportation Science at the Department of Technology, Management and Economics at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU).

My research lies at the intersection of behavioural modelling, simulation and machine learning with a focus on the design, management and enhancement of intelligent, human-centric and sustainable mobility systems. A central aspect of my research is the integration of advanced mathematical models, algorithms and data collection methods-such as immersive virtual reality-to improve our ability to understand and predict human behaviour and decision-making in the context of transport and mobility. The practical applications of my work include emerging transportation technologies, travel demand forecasting, multimodal systems, mobility-on-demand and the interplay between mobility, sustainability, health and wellbeing.

I received a European Research Council Starting Grant for my project IMMERSION: Explaining human decision-making by combining choice and process Data (2024–2029).

I serve on the editorial board of Transportation Research Part A: Policy and Practice.

Before joining DTU, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) in Switzerland. I hold a PhD in Civil and Environmental Engineering from UNSW Sydney, Australia.
