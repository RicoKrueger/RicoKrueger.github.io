About

I am an assistant professor in the Intelligent Transportation Systems Section in the Division of Transportation Science at the Department of Techonology, Management and Economics at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU).

My research interests are in transport and mobility with a particular focus on human behaviour. Recent application areas of my interdisciplinary research programme include:

  • travel demand forecasting,
  • multimodal transportation systems,
  • mobility on-demand,
  • emerging technologies,
  • sustainability,
  • health and well-being.

My recent work involves the development and application of the following methods:

  • machine learning,
  • discrete choice analysis,
  • econometrics,
  • statistics,
  • Bayesian methods,
  • activity-based models.

Prior to joining DTU, I was a postdoc in the Transport and Mobility Laboratory (TRANSP-OR) at the Ecole Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland. I completed my PhD in Civil and Environmental Engineering at UNSW Australia.

I serve on the editorial board of Transportation Research Part A: Policy and Practice.
