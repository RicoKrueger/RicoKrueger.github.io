About me

I am a postdoc in the Transport and Mobility Laboratory (TRANSP-OR) at Ecole Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) under the direction of Michel Bierlaire.

My research focusses on the development of methods at the intersection of machine learning, econometrics and statistics with applications in transport systems. I am especially interested in

  1. probabilistic modelling and Bayesian inference,
  2. using machine learning to assist the specification of behavioural and statistical models, and
  3. analysing transport demand with a particular emphasis on emerging and sustainable transport technologies.

Prior to joining EPFL, I completed a PhD in Civil & Environmental Engineering at UNSW Australia under the supervision of Taha H. Rashidi, S. Travis Waller and Akshay Vij. I hold MSc and BSc degrees in Industrial Engineering & Management from TU Berlin.
